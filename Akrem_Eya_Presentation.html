Analyzing the Effect of AI Manipulation on ESG Reporting Assessment

Chapter 3: Practical Implementation Guide

Researchers: Akrem Majdi & Eya Aouadi

1. Project Objective

The core goal of this study was to analyze how AI-driven language manipulation of a snippet from a Corporate ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) report affects its subsequent automated assessment.

In short: Can we use AI to make a negative or balanced report look purely positive to another AI assessor?

2. Methodology: Baseline vs. Manipulated

Baseline Snippet

  • Content: Original, objective text.
  • Characteristics: Included positive claims, but also qualifiers and mentions of ongoing challenges (e.g., moderate emission increase).

Manipulated Snippet (via ChatGPT)

  • Goal: Present only positive achievements.
  • Key Changes: Removed qualifiers ("however," "we recognize").
  • Key Changes: Removed mentions of absolute emission increases.
  • Key Changes: Used a more celebratory and definitive tone.

5. Conclusion and Key Takeaway: The AI Greenwashing Effect

  • The Quantitative Impact: A simple linguistic manipulation increased the ESG score by **8 points** and lowered the risk from **Medium to Low**.
  • AI Vulnerability: The study suggests that AI-driven text analysis, when performed solely on a snippet, is vulnerable to manipulation techniques (a form of "AI-optimized greenwashing").
  • Language is Powerful: Manipulations like using celebratory language and removing qualifiers directly and positively influenced the AI's assessment.
  • Context Matters: By simply removing mentions of absolute emission increases and challenges, the report appeared to be a flawless performance, despite the underlying data gaps.
  • Final Result: Language manipulation created a perception of better overall environmental performance without requiring any concrete policy or real-world improvement.
